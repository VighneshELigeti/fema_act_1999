Chapter 09

Contraventions & Penalties

Section 13 is FEMA's penalty provision. A fundamental shift from FERA: violations are civil wrongs, not criminal offences. The focus is on monetary penalties, not imprisonment.

⚖️
Civil vs Criminal — The Key Distinction: Under FERA 1973, forex violations were criminal offences — police could arrest you, courts could imprison you, and the burden of proof was on the accused ("guilty until proven innocent"). FEMA changed ALL of this to civil proceedings — monetary penalties only, burden of proof on the prosecution, quasi-judicial process with natural justice principles.

Section 13 — Penalty Structure

Maximum penalties for FEMA contraventions

💰
3× Amount

Maximum penalty where the amount of contravention is quantifiable — up to three times the sum involved

📋
₹2 Lakh

Where amount is NOT quantifiable — maximum ₹2 lakh penalty (e.g., failure to file a return, procedural violation)

📅
₹5,000/day

Additional daily penalty if the contravention continues after the order is passed (continuing default)

What Constitutes a FEMA Contravention?

🚫

Dealing in forex without authorization (Section 3)

Buying, selling, or transferring forex to/from anyone other than an authorized person. Hawala transactions, unauthorized money transfer operators, crypto-based forex dealing.

🚫

Making payment to non-residents without RBI permission

Capital account payments without proper authorization — e.g., investing abroad without LRS/ODI route, lending money to a foreign entity without ECB framework.

🚫

Non-repatriation of export proceeds (Section 7)

Exporter fails to bring back forex within 9 months (or extended timeline). Very common contravention — RBI's EDPMS system automatically flags overdue bills.

🚫

Violation of FDI pricing guidelines

Issuing shares to non-residents below fair value, or buyback of shares above fair value. Pricing guidelines differ for listed (market price) vs unlisted (DCF or other acceptable method).

🚫

Delayed or non-filing of FC-GPR/FC-TRS/FLA/APR

Procedural non-compliance — not filing required reports with RBI within prescribed timelines. Technical but still a contravention.

🚫

Breach of sectoral FDI caps

Foreign investment exceeding the prescribed sectoral limit — e.g., more than 74% FDI in insurance without specific approval, or any FDI in prohibited sectors like gambling, lotteries.

🚫

Taking up or holding out as an Authorized Person without authorization

Operating as a money changer, forex dealer, or remittance agent without RBI's authorization under Section 10.

Director & Officer Liability — Section 42

👤
Section 42: Where a company contravenes FEMA, every person who was in charge of and responsible for the company's business at the time of the contravention is also deemed guilty and liable for penalty. This means directors, CFOs, company secretaries, and compliance officers can be personally penalized. However, they can escape if they prove the contravention occurred without their knowledge or that they exercised due diligence.

ED's Investigation Powers

ED can issue summons requiring any person to produce documents or give evidence. This includes bank statements, emails, contracts, minutes of board meetings — essentially any document ED considers relevant. Non-compliance with summons is itself a contravention.

ED can search any premises (with authorization from a competent authority) and seize documents, cash, and other property suspected to be connected with FEMA violations. They can also seal premises. Personal search of individuals is done by an officer of the same gender.

The Director of Enforcement can provisionally attach any property of a person who has been issued a show cause notice, if there's reason to believe the person may dissipate assets to avoid penalty. Attachment can last up to 180 days, extendable by the Adjudicating Authority.

Many FEMA violations also trigger investigation under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), 2002 — which IS criminal law. ED handles both. So while FEMA itself is civil, the same transaction may attract criminal investigation under PMLA if money laundering is suspected.
