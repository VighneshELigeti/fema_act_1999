Chapter 02

Scope & Applicability

Who does FEMA apply to? What transactions does it cover? And why "residential status" is the most important concept in FEMA.

Territorial Scope

FEMA extends to the whole of India. But here's the catch — it also applies to:

🇮🇳
All of India
(including J&K)
🏢
Offices / Branches
outside India owned by Indian residents
🌍
Any person
outside India who contravenes FEMA
💡
Extra-territorial reach: If you're an Indian resident with a branch in London, FEMA applies to that branch's forex transactions too. And if a person sitting in Dubai contravenes FEMA provisions, they can be penalized under FEMA when they come to India or through their assets in India.

Who Does FEMA Apply To?

FEMA doesn't just regulate "citizens" — it regulates based on residential status. This is fundamentally different from income tax residency. Under FEMA, your status as "resident" or "non-resident" determines what forex transactions you can do.

🎯 FEMA covers these categories of persons

Residential Status — The Trigger

🔑
This is THE most important concept in FEMA. Your residential status under FEMA determines which rules apply to you. And it's different from income tax residency — don't mix them up!

Person Resident in India (Section 2(v))

You are a "person resident in India" if you have lived in India for more than 182 days during the preceding financial year AND are not a person who has gone out to stay outside India for purposes like:

  • Employment outside India
  • Carrying on business or vocation outside India
  • Any other purpose indicating intention to stay outside India for an uncertain period

Person Resident Outside India (Section 2(w))

Anyone who is NOT a person resident in India is a "person resident outside India" (commonly called NRI / Non-Resident).

FEMA Residency vs. Income Tax Residency

Parameter FEMA Residency Income Tax Residency
Legal basis FEMA Section 2(v) & 2(w) Income Tax Act Section 6
Day count 182 days in preceding FY Multiple tests: 182 days in current FY OR 60 days in current FY + 365 in 4 preceding FYs
Intention matters? Yes — purpose of going abroad matters (employment, business, uncertain period) No — purely mathematical day-count
Citizenship relevance Not directly relevant — based on residence Special rules for Indian citizens leaving India
Can person be resident under one and non-resident under the other? Yes! This happens frequently and creates practical compliance complexity.

Common Scenarios — Who is Resident?

👩‍💻

IT Professional in Bangalore

Works in India full-time. Lives in India throughout the year.

✓ Resident in India
👨‍⚕️

Doctor Working in Dubai

Indian citizen, but moved to Dubai 3 years ago for employment. Visits India for 30 days/year.

↗ Non-Resident (PROI)
🎓

Student Studying Abroad

Indian student doing a 2-year MBA in USA. Left India 8 months ago.

↗ Non-Resident (PROI)
🏢

MNC with India Office

US company with a registered branch office in Mumbai. The branch itself is treated as:

✓ Resident in India
🇬🇧

British Citizen Working in India

UK national, posted to India for a 3-year assignment. Has been in India for 200 days this FY.

✓ Resident in India
💼

Indian Businessman — Frequent Traveller

Indian citizen running an export business. Travels 200 days/year but comes back to India base.

✓ Resident in India (base is in India)
⚠️
Companies have their own rules: An Indian company (incorporated in India under Companies Act) is ALWAYS a person resident in India under FEMA, regardless of who owns it or where its directors sit. A company incorporated outside India is a person resident outside India — even if 100% of its revenue comes from India.

What Transactions Does FEMA Cover?

FEMA regulates any transaction that involves:

💱 Foreign Exchange
Any currency other than INR, traveller's cheques, drafts denominated in forex
📄 Foreign Security
Shares, bonds, debentures, GDRs issued outside India or in foreign currency
🏠 Immovable Property
Acquisition/transfer of property outside India by residents (or in India by non-residents)
💳 Cross-Border Payments
Any payment to or from a person outside India, in rupees or forex

Section 3 — The Heart of FEMA

📌
Section 3 is the foundation provision. It says: "No person shall deal in or transfer any foreign exchange or foreign security to any person not being an authorized person... no person shall make any payment to or for the credit of any person resident outside India in any manner... except through an authorized person."

In plain English: All forex transactions must go through authorized channels (banks). You cannot deal in forex informally — no hawala, no cash forex with unauthorized dealers.
