Chapter 12

Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)

LRS is arguably the most relevant FEMA provision for individual Indian residents. Introduced in 2004, it allows resident individuals to freely remit up to a specified limit per financial year for any permissible current or capital account transaction.

$250,000
Per person per financial year (April–March)

≈ ₹2.1 crore at current exchange rates

📜
Legal basis: LRS was introduced by RBI under its regulation-making powers. The limit has been revised multiple times: USD 25,000 (2004) → USD 50,000 → USD 100,000 → USD 200,000 → USD 250,000 (current, since 2015). During the 2008 crisis, it was temporarily reduced to USD 50,000.

🚫 What LRS CANNOT Be Used For

  • Remittance for purchase of lottery tickets, sweepstakes, banned magazines (Schedule I prohibition)
  • Remittance to countries identified as "non-cooperative" by FATF
  • Trading in forex abroad (speculative forex trading overseas)
  • Remittance for margins or margin calls to overseas exchanges/counterparties
  • Remittance for buying FCCBs issued by Indian companies in secondary market abroad
  • Capital account remittances directly or indirectly to countries where FEMA restricts (e.g., Pakistan — different rules apply)

Tax Collected at Source (TCS) on LRS

Since October 2023, TCS rates have been revised significantly:

PurposeUp to ₹7 lakh/FYAbove ₹7 lakh/FY
Education (funded by loan)Nil0.5%
Education (self-funded)Nil5%
Medical treatmentNil5%
All other purposes (travel, investment, gifts, etc.)Nil20%
Tour packages (any amount)5% (no ₹7 lakh threshold — TCS from Re. 1)
💡
TCS is NOT a tax liability — it's an advance. TCS collected is adjustable against your total income tax liability when you file your ITR. If your total tax payable is less than TCS collected, you get a refund. But it does lock up your money until the ITR is processed — so factor this into your cash flow planning.
