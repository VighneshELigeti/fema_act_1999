Chapter 10

Adjudication & Appeals

FEMA establishes a structured, multi-tier dispute resolution mechanism. Each level provides an opportunity for review, ensuring fairness and preventing arbitrary penalties.

Adjudication Process — Step by Step

Day 0
Show Cause Notice (SCN): The Adjudicating Authority issues SCN to the person, specifying the alleged contravention and demanding explanation.
30 days
Written reply: The person must file a written reply to the SCN within 30 days (extendable).
Hearing
Personal hearing: The person gets an opportunity to be heard — personally or through an authorized representative (lawyer/CA). This is a fundamental right of natural justice.
Evidence
Evidence examination: The AA examines documents, statements, and evidence produced by both ED (prosecution) and the person (defence).
Order
Adjudication order: The AA passes a reasoned order — either imposing penalty or discharging the person. Must record findings of fact and law.
45 days
Appeal window: Aggrieved party can appeal within 45 days to Special Director (if penalty ≤ ₹5 lakh) or directly to Appellate Tribunal (if penalty > ₹5 lakh).

Principles of Natural Justice in FEMA Adjudication

📜 Right to Notice

No person can be penalized without first being given a show cause notice specifying the exact nature of the alleged contravention and the evidence against them.

🗣️ Right to be Heard

Every person has the right to a personal hearing before the AA. They can appear personally, through a lawyer, CA, or authorized representative.

📝 Reasoned Order

The AA must pass a reasoned order explaining the findings of fact, application of law, and justification for the penalty amount. Unreasoned orders can be set aside on appeal.

⚖️ Burden of Proof

Unlike FERA (where accused had to prove innocence), under FEMA the burden is on the prosecution (ED/AA) to prove the contravention. Standard of proof: preponderance of probability (civil standard).

🔄 No Bias

The AA must be impartial — "nemo judex in causa sua" (no one should be judge in their own cause). If bias is demonstrated, the order can be challenged.

📋 Cross-Examination

The person can cross-examine witnesses produced by ED. Reliance on statements without providing opportunity for cross-examination has been held to vitiate proceedings.

Critical Time Limits

⏰
  • Reply to SCN: 30 days from receipt (extendable)
  • Appeal to Special Director: 45 days from date of order
  • Appeal to Appellate Tribunal: 45 days from date of order
  • Appeal to High Court: 60 days from date of Tribunal order
  • Condonation of delay: All appellate authorities can condone delay if "sufficient cause" is shown
  • Limitation for initiating proceedings: SCN must be issued within 2 years of contravention (though interpretation varies)
