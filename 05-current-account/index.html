Chapter 05

Current Account Transactions

Section 5 of FEMA is the gateway provision for current account transactions. The default rule is freedom — any person may sell or draw foreign exchange for a current account transaction, unless specifically restricted by the Central Government.

✅
The Golden Rule of Section 5: "Any person may sell or draw foreign exchange to or from an authorised person if such sale or drawal is a current account transaction." This is FEMA's most liberalizing provision — current account is FREE by default. Only specific carved-out transactions need approval or are prohibited.

The Three Schedules — Classification of Restrictions

The Foreign Exchange Management (Current Account Transactions) Rules, 2000 classify restricted transactions into three schedules:

Prohibited

Schedule I — Transactions That Are Totally Prohibited

These transactions can NEVER be done, no matter what.

  • Remittance for lottery/sweepstakes winnings — Can't send money abroad to buy lottery tickets or receive lottery prizes
  • Remittance of income from racing/riding — Income from horse racing, sports gambling etc.
  • Remittance for purchase of lottery tickets, banned magazines, football pools, sweepstakes
  • Payment of commission on exports under Rupee State Credit Route (except to Nepal/Bhutan)
  • Remittance for travel to Nepal/Bhutan — Uses INR, not forex (special bilateral arrangement)
  • Transactions with persons or entities in countries listed by FATF as non-cooperative
Prior Approval Required — Central Govt

Schedule II — Requires Central Government Permission

These are allowed but only with prior approval of the relevant Ministry.

  • Cultural tours — Ministry of Human Resources Development approval
  • Advertisement in foreign print media for purposes other than promotion of tourism, FDI, and international bidding (exceeding USD 10,000) — Needs approval
  • Remittance of freight by vessel chartered from a person resident outside India
  • Payment of import licenses fee — As per DGFT guidelines
  • Multi-modal transport operators' payments — If above threshold
RBI Approval / Capped

Schedule III — Requires RBI Approval Beyond Limits

These can be done freely up to certain limits. RBI approval needed only for amounts beyond those limits.

  • Private visits abroad — Up to USD 250,000 per FY under LRS; beyond = RBI approval
  • Gift / Donation remittance — Up to USD 250,000 per FY under LRS
  • Going abroad for employment — Up to USD 250,000 per FY
  • Emigration expenses — Up to USD 250,000 per FY
  • Maintenance of close relatives abroad — Up to USD 250,000 per FY
  • Business travel — Up to USD 250,000 per FY
  • Medical treatment abroad — Up to USD 250,000 (plus additional USD 250,000 against estimate from doctor/hospital)
  • Studies abroad — Up to USD 250,000 per academic year (or estimate from institution, whichever is higher)

Common Transaction Buckets

✈️

Foreign Travel

Up to USD 250,000/FY under LRS
  • Can buy forex from AD-I or AD-II
  • Forex for Nepal/Bhutan in INR only
  • International debit/credit card usage counts separately (up to USD 250,000 under LRS for card usage too from 2023-24)
🎓

Education Abroad

USD 250,000/academic year or actual estimate
  • AD bank can release without RBI approval up to limit
  • Beyond limit: release against estimate from institution
  • Covers tuition, hostel, living expenses
  • Education loan remittances also covered
🏥

Medical Treatment Abroad

USD 250,000 + additional estimate-based
  • Base USD 250,000 under LRS
  • Additional: against estimate from hospital/doctor
  • Covers treatment, boarding, travel of patient + attendant
📦

Imports

As per trade limit / LC terms
  • Payment through AD bank against shipping documents
  • Advance remittance for imports allowed with conditions
  • Must ensure goods are received
  • Imports must comply with DGFT/Customs rules
👨‍👩‍👧

Family Maintenance

Up to USD 250,000/FY under LRS
  • Remittance for living expenses of spouse, children, parents abroad
  • No RBI approval if within LRS limit
  • AD bank processes against KYC documents
🎁

Gifts & Donations

Up to USD 250,000/FY under LRS
  • Can remit gifts to non-residents
  • Donations to foreign organizations allowed within limit
  • Beyond limit requires RBI approval
💡
Everything else is FREE: Remember, Schedules I–III cover only the EXCEPTIONS. Any current account transaction NOT listed in these schedules is completely free — you can simply approach your AD bank and transact. This is the "negative list" approach: only what's listed is restricted; everything else is permitted.
