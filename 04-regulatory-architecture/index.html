Chapter 04

Regulatory Architecture

FEMA creates a multi-layered institutional framework where Parliament legislates, the Central Government rulemaking, RBI regulates day-to-day, and enforcement is separated from adjudication.

🏛️
Key design principle: FEMA intentionally separates the power to make rules (RBI/Central Govt), the power to investigate (Enforcement Directorate), the power to adjudicate (Adjudicating Authority), and the power to hear appeals (Special Director/Appellate Tribunal/High Court). This separation prevents arbitrary action.

Key Powers & Functions

    RBI — The Principal Regulator

    RBI is the most important day-to-day regulator under FEMA. Here's what it does:

    RBI authorizes banks and financial institutions to deal in forex (Authorized Dealers), money changers, and OBUs. It can revoke authorization for non-compliance. Without RBI's license, no entity can deal in foreign exchange.

    RBI can issue directions to authorized persons regarding forex dealings, require information/documents, inspect books/accounts, and generally ensure APs comply with FEMA. This is the backbone of operational control — RBI issues circulars to AD banks who then enforce limits at the ground level.

    RBI can make regulations for all matters under FEMA that Parliament has delegated to it. These regulations (like FEMA 20 for FDI, FEMA 7/8 for current/capital account) have the force of law. They're published in the Official Gazette. Most practical FEMA compliance revolves around these regulations.

    RBI can cause an inspection of any authorized person, its books, accounts, and other documents. RBI can appoint inspectors who have powers similar to a civil court. This ensures authorized persons properly follow FEMA requirements.

    Authorized Person Categories

    Under Section 10, RBI creates a tiered system of authorized persons:

    AD Category I

    Commercial Banks

    All scheduled commercial banks (SBI, HDFC, ICICI etc.). Can handle ALL forex transactions — trade, remittances, capital account, LRS, ECBs, FDI, everything. They're the primary forex interface for most people and businesses.

    AD Category II

    Upgraded FFMCs

    Full Fledged Money Changers upgraded to AD-II. Limited transactions: non-trade current account (travel, education, medical), release of forex for private visits, and certain trade-related transactions up to specified limits.

    AD Category III

    Select Financial Institutions

    Certain FIs authorized for specific purposes (e.g., NABARD, EXIM Bank, SIDBI). Very limited scope — only specific transactions they're authorized for. Not general-purpose forex dealers.

    Enforcement & Adjudication Process

    When FEMA is violated, here's the step-by-step process:

    Detection
    By RBI, ED, or reported by AD bank
    →
    2
    Investigation

    →
    3

    Issued by Adjudicating Authority

    7

    Within 45 days

    8
    High Court
    On questions of law only
    ⚖️
    FEMA is civil, not criminal: Unlike FERA (which was criminal), FEMA contraventions attract only civil penalties — monetary fines, not imprisonment. However, if the violation also amounts to an offence under other laws (like Prevention of Money Laundering Act), criminal consequences can follow through those separate laws.
