Chapter 14

Amendments & Recent Developments

FEMA has been continuously updated through amendments, new regulations, RBI circulars, and policy changes. This chapter tracks the major milestones from 2000 to the present.

Major Amendments & Milestones

2000

FEMA Comes Into Force (1 June 2000)

FEMA replaced FERA. Initial set of regulations issued: FEMA 1-23 covering all major areas — current account, capital account, deposits, FDI, export/import, authorized persons etc. India officially moved from "regulation" to "management" of forex.

2002

PMLA Enacted — FEMA Interface Created

Prevention of Money Laundering Act 2002 designated FEMA offences (above ₹30 lakh) as scheduled offences. This created the FEMA-PMLA nexus that makes serious forex violations potentially criminal.

2004

LRS Introduced

Liberalised Remittance Scheme launched with initial limit of USD 25,000 per person per FY. Landmark step in capital account liberalization for individuals — for the first time, resident individuals could remit money abroad without specific RBI approval.

2010

FDI Consolidated Circular

DIPP (now DPIIT) started issuing a single consolidated FDI policy document annually, consolidating all press notes and policy changes. Made FDI framework more transparent and accessible.

2015-16

LRS Limit Raised to USD 250,000

LRS limit progressively increased and stabilized at USD 250,000. RBI also rationalized several FEMA regulations, simplifying compliance.

2015

FEMA Amendment Act, 2015

Major legislative amendment: introduced provisions for establishment of ADT (Appellate Tribunal), enhanced compounding provisions, empowered Central Government to prescribe civil penalties, and strengthened enforcement provisions. Also added sections on cross-border merger framework.

2017

FEMA 20(R) — Consolidated FDI Regulation

Replaced the old FEMA 20/2000 with a comprehensive, re-written regulation covering all aspects of FDI — entry routes, pricing, reporting, downstream investment, transfer of shares, etc. Single-window reference for all FDI compliance.

2018-19

"R" Series — Regulation Consolidation

RBI replaced multiple old FEMA regulations with consolidated "R" versions: FEMA 3R (ECBs), FEMA 5R (deposits), FEMA 7R/8R (current/capital account), FEMA 21R (immovable property). Massive simplification exercise.

April 2020

Press Note 3 (2020) — Land Border FDI Restrictions

In response to Chinese companies reportedly trying to acquire stakes in Indian firms during the COVID-19 market downturn, the government mandated prior government approval for FDI from countries sharing a land border with India (China, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Nepal, Myanmar, Bhutan, Afghanistan). Still in effect.

August 2022

New Overseas Investment Rules & Regulations

Completely revamped the overseas investment framework: new FEMA (Overseas Investment) Rules, 2022 and FEMA (Overseas Investment) Regulations, 2022. Replaced the old ODI regulations. Introduced the concept of "financial commitment" (equity + debt + guarantee + non-fund-based), simplified approval routes, and clarified round-tripping provisions.

2023

TCS on LRS — Major Tax Change

Finance Act 2023 introduced 20% TCS on LRS remittances above ₹7 lakh (except education/medical at 5%). International credit card spends brought under LRS. Significant impact on individual remittances — though TCS is adjustable against income tax.

2023-24

RBI Master Directions Updates

RBI continued converting old circulars into consolidated Master Directions. Key updates: revised Master Direction on LRS, updated FEMA reporting requirements, new directions on export/import with Asian Clearing Union members, liberalized ECB framework further.

Recent & Notable Developments

2023-24

Rupee Trade Settlement

RBI allowed rupee-denominated trade settlement with certain countries (Russia, UAE, etc.). Indian banks can open Special Vostro Rupee Accounts (SVRAs) for correspondent banks of partner countries. Reduces USD dependency for bilateral trade.

2023

GIFT City Liberalization

Gujarat International Finance Tec-City (GIFT IFSC) received significant FEMA relaxations — lighter regulatory regime, international financial services center with special rules for offshore banking, insurance, and fund management.

Ongoing

Digital & Crypto Framework

While crypto is not explicitly covered under FEMA, forex aspects of crypto transactions fall under FEMA purview. RBI has introduced CBDC (e₹) pilot. Clarity on FEMA applicability to crypto remains evolving.

2024

FDI Policy Reforms

Continued liberalization of FDI sectors — space sector opened to 100% FDI, insurance cap raised to 74%, defence sector opened further. India remains one of the most FDI-friendly regimes among large emerging economies.

🔮 The Road Ahead — What to Watch

  • Full Capital Account Convertibility: The long-term goal per Tarapore Committee recommendations. India may gradually move towards full convertibility as forex reserves grow and macroeconomic stability strengthens.
  • Digital/Crypto FEMA framework: As cross-border digital assets grow, FEMA will need formal provisions for crypto, DeFi, and digital asset transfers.
  • CBDC Integration: RBI's digital rupee (e₹) may change how cross-border payments work under FEMA — potential for faster, cheaper international settlements.
  • Rupee Internationalization: With rupee trade settlement expanding, FEMA may need amendments to support INR as an international trade currency.
  • Simplification: RBI continues to simplify regulation by consolidating Master Directions, automating reporting (moving from forms to API-based reporting), and reducing compliance burden.
  • Land border FDI review: Press Note 3 (2020) may be revised as geopolitical dynamics evolve — watch for any relaxation of the mandatory approval requirement for Chinese investment.
