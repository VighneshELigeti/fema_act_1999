Chapter 11

FEMA Interfaces with Other Laws

FEMA doesn't operate in isolation. It intersects with income tax, company law, PMLA, SEBI regulations, customs, and more. Understanding these interfaces is crucial for complete compliance.

🏦 FEMA × Income Tax Act

→

The most critical interface. Key overlaps:

  • Residential status divergence: FEMA and IT Act have DIFFERENT tests for "resident." You can be a FEMA non-resident but IT resident (or vice versa). FEMA focuses on 182 days + intention; IT focuses on days of physical presence with different counting rules.
  • Transfer pricing: FDI/ODI transactions must comply with both FEMA pricing guidelines (fair value via DCF etc.) AND IT Act's transfer pricing rules (arm's length price). The two may give different values — creating compliance risk.
  • TDS on forex payments: Payments to non-residents require TDS under Section 195 of IT Act. AD banks may require Form 15CA/15CB (CA certificate + online declaration) before remitting forex.
  • Foreign asset reporting: Black Money Act 2015 requires Indian tax residents to disclose foreign assets — overlaps with FEMA's foreign investment reporting.
  • DTAA benefits: Double Tax Avoidance Agreements affect withholding rates on cross-border payments, which interact with FEMA remittance processes.

🔒 FEMA × Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA)

→

The most feared interface. PMLA is criminal law — violation means arrest and imprisonment.

  • Scheduled offence: FEMA contraventions involving an amount > ₹30 lakh are designated as "scheduled offences" under PMLA. This means ED can investigate under both FEMA (civil) and PMLA (criminal) simultaneously.
  • Proceeds of crime: If FEMA violation generates "proceeds of crime" (money derived from the illegal activity), PMLA attachment and confiscation provisions apply.
  • Same investigator: ED handles both FEMA and PMLA investigations. A FEMA inquiry can easily escalate to PMLA if money laundering is suspected.
  • Bail provisions: PMLA has strict bail provisions — the accused must demonstrate that they are not guilty AND not likely to commit the offence. This is opposite of normal criminal law where bail is the rule.

🏢 FEMA × Companies Act 2013

→
  • Share issuance: Allotment of shares to non-residents must comply with both Companies Act (statutory requirements, board/shareholder resolutions, ROC filings) AND FEMA (FC-GPR, pricing, sectoral caps).
  • Annual compliance: Companies with foreign investment must file Annual Return with ROC AND FLA Return with RBI.
  • Significant Beneficial Ownership: Companies Act SBO provisions interact with FEMA's beneficial ownership tracking for FDI.
  • Director appointments: Non-resident directors require DIN and must comply with both Companies Act requirements and FEMA regulations regarding remuneration/payments.

📈 FEMA × SEBI Regulations

→
  • FPI Regulations: Foreign Portfolio Investors must register with SEBI AND comply with FEMA 20(R) limits. SEBI regulates registration and conduct; FEMA regulates the investment caps.
  • Takeover Code: SEBI's Takeover Code applies when FDI crosses certain thresholds in listed companies — creates additional compliance layers beyond FEMA.
  • Listing regulations: Companies with FDI/FPI must comply with SEBI LODR for continuous disclosure, alongside FEMA reporting.
  • P-Notes: Participatory Notes issued by FPIs are regulated by both SEBI and FEMA — separate compliance requirements apply.

📦 FEMA × Customs Act / DGFT

→
  • Export-import: Every export/import involves FEMA (forex flow) + Customs Act (duty, clearance) + DGFT (licensing, policy). GR/SDF forms are filed with customs AND AD bank.
  • Gold import: Customs duty + FEMA restrictions — only nominated agencies can import gold.
  • EDPMS/IDPMS: RBI's electronic monitoring system links customs data (shipping bills/bills of entry) with banking data (forex transactions) to track trade compliance.
  • Free Trade Agreements: DGFT issues licenses; FEMA ensures forex flows correctly; Customs verifies origin and applies duty rates.

🏠 FEMA × RERA / State Laws

→
  • NRI property: NRIs buying property in India must comply with FEMA 21R (immovable property rules) AND state-level RERA registration, stamp duty, and registration laws.
  • Agricultural land: FEMA generally prohibits non-residents from buying agricultural land, farmhouse, or plantation property. State laws may have additional restrictions for non-residents.
  • Repatriation of sale proceeds: When NRIs sell property, FEMA limits repatriation (e.g., max 2 residential properties, original investment must have been in forex).
