Chapter 06

Capital Account Transactions

Section 6 governs capital account transactions — the most regulated part of FEMA. Unlike current account (free by default), capital account operates on a "positive list" approach: only what RBI/Central Government specifically permits is allowed.

🔐
Section 6(2) & 6(3) — The Framework: The Central Government may, in consultation with RBI, prescribe classes of capital account transactions that are permissible, limits, and conditions. RBI may also specify permissible transactions with conditions. India follows progressive capital account liberalization — not full convertibility, but gradually opening up.

Major Capital Account Transaction Types

🏢
Automatic + Approval

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI)

Investment by non-residents into Indian companies. Most sectors allow 100% FDI under automatic route. Sensitive sectors (defence, media, banking) need government approval. Governed by FEMA 20(R)/2017.

🌍
Mixed Routes

Overseas Direct Investment (ODI)

Investment by Indian residents into foreign companies. Under FEMA (Overseas Investment) Rules, 2022. Can invest up to 400% of net worth under automatic route. Financial commitment includes equity, loan, and guarantee.

📊
SEBI Regulated

Foreign Portfolio Investment (FPI)

Short-term investment in Indian securities by registered FPIs. Governed by FEMA 20(R) and SEBI (FPI) Regulations, 2019. Subject to aggregate and individual limits for listed companies.

💰
RBI Approval Framework

External Commercial Borrowings (ECB)

Borrowing from foreign lenders. Automatic route up to USD 750 million per FY. Minimum average maturity requirements. All-in-cost ceiling linked to benchmark rates. FEMA 3R/2018.

🏠
Specific Rules

Immovable Property

NRIs/PIOs can buy residential/commercial property in India (not agricultural). Indian residents can acquire immovable property outside India under certain conditions (inheritance, purchase under ODI/LRS). FEMA 21R/2018.

🤝
Automatic with Limits

Guarantees

Indian residents can give guarantees for foreign entities and vice versa. Subject to specific conditions. Corporate guarantees for overseas subsidiaries governed by ODI rules.

💱
RBI Directions

Forex Derivatives

Hedging contracts, forward covers, options, swaps. AD Category I banks can offer these. Users must have underlying exposure. Speculative derivatives trading generally not permitted.

🏦
NRI/PIO Specific

NRI Deposits (NRE/NRO/FCNR)

NRE: fully repatriable, tax-free interest. NRO: limited repatriation (up to USD 1 mn/FY), interest taxable. FCNR(B): foreign currency term deposits, fully repatriable. FEMA 5R/2017.

FDI — Automatic vs Approval Route

✅ Automatic Route

No prior government approval needed. Just inform RBI through AD bank within 30 days.

  • Most manufacturing sectors — 100%
  • IT/ITES — 100%
  • E-commerce (marketplace model) — 100%
  • Construction development — 100%
  • Infrastructure — 100%
  • Mining — 100% (most minerals)
  • Services sector — 100%
  • Healthcare — 100%
  • Tourism & Hospitality — 100%

⚠️ Government Approval Route

Needs approval from concerned Ministry/Department. Proposals on Foreign Investment Facilitation Portal (FIFP).

  • Multi-brand retail — 51% (with conditions)
  • Broadcasting (news) — 49%
  • Print media (news) — 26%
  • Mining & mineral separation of titanium — 100%
  • Core investment companies — Approval beyond 49%
  • Food products retail — 100% (with conditions)
  • Investment from countries sharing land border (China, etc.) — all sectors need approval

ECB Framework — Key Parameters

Companies, LLPs, SEZ units, SIDBI, EXIM Bank, port trusts, housing finance companies, NBFCs, microfinance institutions, registered entities. NOT: individuals, trusts, partnerships (other than LLPs), or government entities.

Track I (up to USD 50 mn): 3 years MAM. Track II (above USD 50 mn): 5 years MAM. Track III (INR denominated): 3-5 years depending on amount. Manufacturing sector sometimes gets relaxed MAM of 1 year for smaller amounts.

The total cost of borrowing (interest + other charges) must not exceed benchmark rate + spread. For example, SOFR + 550 bps. This prevents Indian companies from borrowing at excessively high rates which could indicate round-tripping.

ECB proceeds CAN be used for: capital expenditure, working capital (with limits), repayment of Rupee loans, overseas investment. CANNOT be used for: real estate (except affordable housing), on-lending to related entities (unless permitted), equity investment in India, purchase of land.

📈
India's Capital Account Liberalization Journey: India moved from near-zero capital account openness in 1991 to significant liberalization today. But full capital account convertibility (like the US or UK) is still a long-term goal. The Tarapore Committee (1997, 2006) laid the roadmap, but India deliberately maintains controls to prevent volatile capital flows from destabilizing the economy.
